Woven Crafts of Ibajay

Threads of Tradition

📍 Barangay Mabusao • Regador • San Jose, Ibajay, Aklan

Weaving in Ibajay is a cultural practice shaped by generations of craftsmanship and tradition. Using nito vines and abaca fibers, artisans create functional and decorative products that reflect skill, patience, and creativity.

Rooted in local heritage, each woven piece highlights the community's connection to nature and sustainable living. The handcrafted works preserve traditional techniques while continuing to serve everyday use.

Bibingka

A beloved Filipino rice cake made with rice flour and coconut milk, traditionally baked in banana leaves. Soft, warm, and slightly sweet, bibingka is often enjoyed during celebrations and family gatherings.

Pinais

A flavorful local delicacy prepared by wrapping rice, coconut milk, and savory ingredients in banana leaves before slow-cooking. Its rich aroma and comforting taste reflect Ibajay’s traditional coastal cuisine.

Ati-Ati Municipal and Devotional Fiesta

Celebrated every January, the Ati-Atihan Festival of Ibajay is a vibrant expression of faith, culture, and unity. It brings the community to life with energetic performances, colorful traditions, and a deep connection to heritage.

History

Ibajay is one of the oldest settlements in the province of Aklan, with roots dating back to the pre-colonial period when early communities thrived along its rivers and coastal areas. The town became an organized municipality during the Spanish era around 1673, serving as an important center for agriculture, trade, and local governance in western Panay.

Through the years, Ibajay expanded as neighboring communities developed, eventually leading to the creation of separate municipalities such as Nabas. Despite these changes, the town preserved its strong cultural identity shaped by faith, craftsmanship, and community traditions.

Historically, Ibajay became known for traditional industries such as nito weaving, abaca fiber production, and pottery, crafts that were passed down through generations and remain part of the municipality’s cultural heritage today.

Today, Ibajay continues to honor its history while embracing growth, standing as a municipality recognized for its heritage, natural landscapes, and enduring local traditions.
